üéØ Types of Adjectives

üé®

Descriptive Adjectives

Words that describe the quality, color, size, or condition of a noun.

Examples: beautiful, tall, red, happy, soft, old
üî¢

Quantitative Adjectives

Words that tell us how much or how many of something there is.

Examples: some, many, few, several, enough, no
üëâ

Demonstrative Adjectives

Words that point out which specific noun we're talking about.

Examples: this, that, these, those
üéÅ

Possessive Adjectives

Words that show ownership or belonging. They tell us whose it is.

Examples: my, your, his, her, its, our, their
‚ùì

Interrogative Adjectives

Words used in questions to modify nouns and ask for specific information.

Examples: which, what, whose
üåç

Proper Adjectives

Adjectives formed from proper nouns. They always start with a capital letter!

Examples: Indian, American, English, Chinese, Italian
