üìñ What is a Verb?

A verb is an action word! It tells us what someone or something is doing. When you run, jump, play, eat, or sleep - these are all verbs! Verbs make sentences exciting because they show action and movement!

‚ú® Examples of Verbs:

  • üèÉ Movement: run, jump, walk, swim, dance, skip
  • üé§ Communication: sing, talk, shout, whisper, read, write
  • üé® Activities: draw, paint, play, build, create, make
  • üò¥ Daily Actions: sleep, eat, drink, think, laugh, smile

üéØ Types of Verbs

‚ö°

Main Verbs

The principal verb that shows the main action or state in a sentence. It carries the main meaning.

Examples: run, eat, sleep, write, dance, think, play, sing
ü§ù

Helping Verbs

Verbs that help the main verb express time, possibility, or necessity. Also called auxiliary verbs.

Examples: is, am, are, was, were, has, have, will, can, should, would, could
